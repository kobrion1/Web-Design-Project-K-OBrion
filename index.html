Care


Taking care of indoor plants involves creating an environment that mimics their natural habitat as closely as possible. Light is one of the most important factors—most houseplants thrive in bright, indirect sunlight, so placing them near a window with filtered light is ideal. Too much direct sun can scorch the leaves, while too little can lead to weak, leggy growth. Watering is another key aspect; it’s best to check the soil regularly and only water when the top inch feels dry. Overwatering is a common mistake and can lead to root rot, so make sure your pots have proper drainage. Check out this website for: How to Dry Out Overwatered Plants






In addition to light and water, indoor plants benefit from a little regular maintenance. Wiping the leaves with a damp cloth keeps them free of dust and allows them to absorb more light. Rotating your plants every couple of weeks helps them grow evenly and prevents them from leaning toward the light source. During the growing season—usually spring and summer—feeding your plants with a diluted, balanced fertilizer every few weeks can promote healthy growth. By giving your plants consistent care and attention, they can flourish and bring a sense of calm and beauty to your indoor space. New Arrivals


The Money Tree

The money tree (Pachira aquatica) is a popular indoor plant known for its braided trunk and lush, green leaves. Believed to bring good luck and prosperity, it’s often used in homes and offices as a symbol of financial success. Easy to care for, the money tree thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers infrequent watering, making it ideal for beginner plant owners. Its unique appearance and low maintenance needs make it a stylish and meaningful addition to any indoor space.
